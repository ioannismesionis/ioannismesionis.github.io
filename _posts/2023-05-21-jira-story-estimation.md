## Agile Estimation

**Agile estimation** is intended to capture:

1. The *amount* of work.
2. The *difficulty* of the work.
3. The *risk inherent* in the work.

When paired with relative estimation, it results in questions like, â€œIs this feature as complicated as the other feature we built last week?â€ Or, â€œIf we take on this feature, is it riskier than the other feature?â€.

When work items are similar enough across these questions, we give them the same number on a scale (see table below) that the team has previously selected. As work items differ, the team discusses those differences to understand just how different the work is, relatively, and gives a corresponding number on that same scale.

## Estimation must be a Team event

A generally accepted practiceÂ is to have the team make estimates together. This practice allows the team to discover how well work is understood, encourages knowledge sharing, builds team cohesiveness, and fosters buy-in. One of the most popular techniques for having these team conversations to obtain estimates is Planning Poker [Cohn 2016]. The gist of it is that the team talks about its work in a structured (but fun) way. The team estimates together, they drive conversation, and they expose uncertainty. And numbers pop out that are just good enough to move forward.
